# User Journey Map

## What problem it solves
User Journey Map reveals friction, expectation gaps, and drop-off points across the end-to-end user experience.

## Best for
- identifying friction across stages
- mapping onboarding, usage, conversion, or support journeys
- linking product pain to specific journey steps

## Not for
- top-level business strategy by itself
- numerical prioritization across unrelated initiatives

## Use when
- the product experience feels fragmented
- users struggle in multiple touchpoints
- the team needs a stage-by-stage view of experience

## Output template
Stage: ______
User goal: ______
Action: ______
Pain point: ______
Emotion / expectation: ______
Opportunity: ______

## Common misuse
Do not draw a pretty journey map without identifying concrete friction and intervention points.
