# HEART

## What problem it solves
HEART helps evaluate user experience quality with a balanced set of experience dimensions.

## Best for
- UX and experience metrics
- product experience health checks
- experience-aware retention analysis

## Not for
- pure business prioritization
- early-stage strategy definition by itself

## Use when
- the team needs a structured experience lens
- users say something feels confusing, frustrating, or low-value
- you want measurable UX guardrails next to product metrics

## Dimensions
- Happiness
- Engagement
- Adoption
- Retention
- Task Success

## Output template
Experience area: ______
Relevant HEART dimension: ______
Measurement idea: ______
Current signal: ______
Desired improvement: ______

## Common misuse
Do not treat HEART as a random metric checklist.
Choose dimensions that actually match the user journey and product stage.
